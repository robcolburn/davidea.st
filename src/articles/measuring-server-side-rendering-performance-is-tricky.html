Hasn't Server-Side Rendering been around... like... forever?

Everyone who has heard the term SSR.

Most things in web development are difficult.

Despite knowing this I'm able to lie to myself at the beginning every project. "This won't be that difficult. Two days tops." But it always happens. I hit a resistance point that forces me to stop and say, "This is gonna take awhile."

I started this post two weeks ago as a simple "How to use SSR to boost performance" article. After hours and profiling and consulting people much smarter than me, I know one thing. Server-side Rendering is more nuanced than you would like.

This is not a "beware SSR" article. SSR can still help with performance in certain scenarios. There's just a few quirks you should be aware of.

JavaScript handcuffs the content of a single-page app

The default setup for a single-page app is an "app" element with some scripts. The "app" element inflates into meaningful content once the scripts run. 

<html>
        <head>
           <title>My non ssr app</title>
        </head>
        <body>
            <my-app></my-app>
            <!-- "my-app" is a white page until these big scripts load -->
            <script src="big-script.876aa678as463sf7.js"></script>
            <script src="other-big-script.876aa678as463sf7.js"></script>
        </body>
     </html>
